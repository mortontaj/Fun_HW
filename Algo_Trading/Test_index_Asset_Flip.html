    • Equities
    This asset class offers ownership in publicly held companies. Historically, this class outperformed other classes over long periods (Nonetheless, past performance does not guarantee future results). Stocks are among the most volatile securities throughout asset classes in the short term. Returns and principal will fluctuate so that accumulations, when redeemed, may be worth more or less than original cost.
    Fixed Income
    This asset class generally pays a set rate of interest over a given period, then returns the investor's principal. Bonds pay interest (ie coupon payments or yield). Bonds offer more stability than stocks in terms of volatility. Value fluctuates due to current interest and inflation rates. They "guarantee risk-free" growth. Includes money market* and infrastructure** instruments (short-term* and long-term** investments)
    Foreign Exchange
    This asset class includes money in any form when in use or circulation as a medium of exchange. Circulating banknotes and coins are currencies. They are traded between national markets. Central banks determine the relative values of currencies. Currencies are defined by governments, and each currency has limited boundaries of acceptance. This includes global decentralized over-the-counter market for trading currencies.

    REITs
    Real Estate Investment Trusts includes your home, investment properties, and commercial RE shares. They help protect future purchasing power as property values and rental income run parallel to inflation. They change slower than stock and bond prices. They are subject to risks from fluctuating property values, expenses, income, and environmental liabilities. Earn dividend income without buying, managing, or financing properties.
    Commodities
    This asset class includes physical goods such as but not limited to gold, copper, crude oil, natural gas, wheat, corn, and even electricity. Helps protect future purchasing power as values have fixed utility and thus run parallel to inflation. Values tend to exhibit low correlations with stock and bond prices. Price dynamics are also unique: these become more volatile as prices rise.
    Derivatives
    This asset class includes contracts that derive value from the performance of underlying entities. This underlying entity can be an asset, index, or interest rate. These help insuring against price movements (hedging), increasing exposure to price movements for speculation, and accessing hard-to-trade assets or markets. ETFs, forwards, futures, options, swaps, synthetic collateralized debt obligations, and credit default swaps.
